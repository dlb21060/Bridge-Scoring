Simplified Chicago Bridge Scoring


Simplified Chicago Bridge Scoring is nearly identical to standard Chicago scoring.


Simplified scoring is particularly useful in situations where players are moving from position to position during the course of a rubber. As in standard Chicago scoring, rubbers are made up of four hands.

Simplified Chicago scoring has also proven to be useful when players with a wide range of skill levels are at the table.


Each hand is independent of previous (and future) hands. That is:

  • Part scores do not carry forward to the following hands. Part scores are awarded a 50 point bonus, as they are in most forms of duplicate bridge. This differs from standard Chicago.

  • Vulnerability is determined by the sequence of deals, and not by whether a pair has scored a previous game. This is the same as in standard Chicago.


Vulnerability and dealer are determined as follows:

  • Deal #1: no one vulnerable; North deals

  • Deal #2: East-West vulnerable; East deals

  • Deal #3: North-South vulnerable; South deals

  • Deal #4: Both pairs vulnerable; West deals.

  • Subsequent deals repeat the pattern above.


Scoring differences from rubber bridge are as follows:

  • Honors points (or honours) are not counted.

  • Part score contracts (below 100 points) which are bid and made earn a 50 point bonus.

  • Game contracts bid and made earn a bonus depending on vulnerability:

    • Non-vul games earn a 300 point bonus.

    • Vulnerable games earn a 500 point bonus.


Other aspects of scoring are identical to scoring in rubber (“standard”) bridge.
